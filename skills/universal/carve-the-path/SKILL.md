# Carve The Path

Create the fewest small end-to-end steps that prove progress. Slice by outcome, not activity, tool, layer, or mood; avoid setup-only steps unless they unblock several later steps.

## Process

1. Extract the outcome, decisions, constraints, non-goals, risks, and evidence.
2. Draft dependency-ordered steps, each with a visible or verifiable result.
3. Mark each `AFK` (agent-ready) or `HITL` (human decision, review, or lived action).
4. Mark durable steps as commit-, log-, or publish-worthy; leave disposable probes as scratch.
5. Check complete coverage and return or publish the path.

```md
# <step>
Type: AFK | HITL
Blocked by: <refs or none>
## Outcome
## End-to-end change
## Touches
## Acceptance criteria
- [ ] Outcome and main failure path are verified
- [ ] Evidence proves the step
- [ ] Non-goals are respected
## Record
<commit, log, publish, or scratch>
## Out of scope
```

Finish with ordered steps, explicit dependencies, AFK/HITL state, evidence coverage, record decisions, and the recommended first step.
